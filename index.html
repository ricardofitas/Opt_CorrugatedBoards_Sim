Optimization Case Matrix

Legend

  • (1) thickness of liners and flute goes from 0.1 to 0.5 mm
  • * thickness of liners and flute are between 0.15 and 0.25 mm
  • 2B / 2C: the design vector has two variables, wavelength and height; B and C correspond to the flute types. Ranges vary according to DIN 55468-1
  • 2 / 4 / 5: the design vector has two, four, or five variables, respectively. The additional two correspond to the thickness of the liners and flute; the last addition is an independent variable representing the thickness of the bottom liner, separated from the top liner
  • CP: NURBS control points
  • 7v(r** > X): the minimum radius of curvature must be higher than X (in mm)
  • H1: 50 particles & 80 iterations (inertia weight = 0.7)
  • H2: 20 particles & 200 iterations (inertia weight = 0.9)
  • H3: 10 particles & 200 iterations (inertia weight = 0.9)

Objective Functions

  • I/λ: Geometric inertia per wavelength (not the true physical moment of inertia). Influences the Edge Crush Test (ECT).
  • A/λ: Cross-sectional area per wavelength. Influences the total weight.
  • Ez: Out-of-plane stiffness. Influences the Flat Crush Test (FCT).
